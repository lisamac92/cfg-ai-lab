The Third Colour — Four Options

Your palette foundation is set: dark navy background + teal accent + white cards. The question is what the third colour is — used exclusively on Table 02 (AI Prospecting) to give it its own identity. Here are four serious options.

Option A
Copper
#B87333 / #D4956A
The classic metallic warm. Copper reads as premium, grounded, and trustworthy — it's the colour of quality hardware and financial tools. Unlike yellow-gold, copper has brown undertones that feel rich rather than cheap. Used by brands like Revolut (warm tier) and many fintech products.

Option B
Warm Coral
#E8553E / #F07A5A
Bold, energetic, and impossible to miss. Coral creates strong contrast against both dark navy and teal — it signals urgency and action, which suits the "find your next prospect" message perfectly. Used by Stripe, Intercom, and many sales-focused SaaS products. More assertive than copper.

Option C
Rich Amber
#D97706 / #F59E0B
This is what gold should look like — deep amber with orange undertones, not the yellow-gold you had before. It reads as warm, confident, and premium without looking cheap. This is the colour used by Tailwind CSS, Linear, and many modern developer tools. The closest to your original intent, done properly.

Electric Violet

Consultant's note

If I were advising you as a client, I would choose Option A — Copper. Here is why: your audience is business owners, not developers. Copper reads as established, premium, and trustworthy — it has the warmth of gold without the cheapness of yellow. It also creates a natural narrative: teal for "live and active" (Receptionist + Workflows) and copper for "intelligence and value" (Prospecting). That said, if you want something bolder and more energetic, Coral (B) is the strongest alternative.
